What Is a Design System? →

Itai Vonshak

Former Material Design lead and critic of rigid rules, the adherence fallacy, and the limits of static system adoption.

Who he is in this corpus

Itai Vonshak, former head of the Material Design team at Google, represents a highly experienced practitioner voicing a critical counter-narrative to design system orthodoxy.1 He argues that design systems have failed to deliver on their core promises, having devolved into rigid rulebooks and glorified sticker sheets that crush innovation and bury organizations under massive maintenance burdens.1

Core positions

  • The Adherence Fallacy. The biggest lie of design systems is that compliance guarantees a good product. Truly great products succeed because of thoughtful design, not because they religiously follow rules.1 See Success Metrics.
  • The "Unread Novels" Problem. Traditional pattern documentation is dead on arrival because no one reads the manuals. When patterns are not encapsulated directly in code, they remain dead text that serves no practical purpose.1 See Adoption, Contribution & Trust.
  • Stifling Innovation. Pre-defined component boxes force designers into visual homogeneity. Designers spend more time searching for which pattern to use than actually solving the user's problem.1
  • Missing Product-Market Fit. Adoption is a massive bottleneck. System teams spend more time selling the system and trying to convince people to adopt it than they do designing it.1
  • Limits of Scale. Systems fail at both ends of the organizational spectrum: small teams lack the bandwidth to maintain documentation, while multi-product teams are forced into centralized compromises that dilute the system's effectiveness.1
  • The Economic Gap. Full design system adoption is a highly expensive proposition. For most organizations, code reuse and design-code alignment are not enough to justify the overhead on cost savings alone.1
  • Dinosaurs in the Age of AI. Design systems remain stuck in the past as static, rigid structures. The next chapter must leverage AI to create dynamic, intelligent, and adaptable designs rather than static rulebooks.1 See Agentic Readiness.
