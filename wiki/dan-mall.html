Dan Mall

The corpus's voice on the human and organizational side — governance, adoption, and value — via the Design System University essays.

Who he is in this corpus

Dan Mall, through the Design System University email essays (2024–2026), is the consultant-practitioner perspective. His recurring move is to reframe technical questions as human ones: adoption is about habits and friction, governance is about trust, value is about business problems — not artifacts.

Core positions

  • Consistency is a bad goal. It's table stakes and a by-product; the real case is the business problem solved (speed, scale, quality).1 See Success Metrics.
  • Governance isn't a flowchart. It's alignment over time built on trust, reducible to a WHO/WHAT/WHEN list discovered by talking to people.1 See Governance.
  • A system team is not a standards committee. Teams focused on reviewing and enforcing have lost the plot.1
  • Remove friction, don't ask for help. Product teams don't contribute because of habit and competing priorities; make contribution the path of least resistance.1 See Adoption, Contribution & Trust.
  • Systems as decision engines. The higher-leverage framing beyond "collection of components."1
  • Keep the savings. Don't let efficiency gains vanish into doubled sprint commitments.1

On AI

Mall also wrote an accessible primer on MCP for design systems, framing Figma's Dev Mode MCP server as giving AI tools "X-ray vision" into a design's real structure.1 See MCP & the Machine-Readable Interface.

Where he sits in the debates

Mall is the strongest voice for trust-based, cultural approaches — a useful counterweight to the enforcement framing of the maturity model and to the "encode governance as hard rules" agentic position. The synthesis across the corpus: encode only what's culturally settled; keep negotiating the rest.
